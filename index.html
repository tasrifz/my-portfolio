Professional Profile

Bridging Scientific Rigor with Enterprise Software Engineering

My transition into software engineering is driven by a core philosophy: great software is built at the intersection of analytical precision and professional discipline. My journey began during my M.Sc. in Zoology at Jagannath University, where I utilized R Programming to dissect complex empirical datasets for my thesis. This experience was my catalyst; it revealed code not just as syntax, but as an ultimate problem-solving engine. Recognizing the limitless potential of technology to solve real-world systemic challenges, I pivoted to professional engineering.

To formalize this analytical baseline into enterprise solutions, I completed an intensive 10-month, 788+ hour residency under the IsDB-BISEW IT Scholarship Programme (Round 68). This program was engineered to rigorously mirror a production-grade IT firm environment. Beyond studying architecture, I mastered the art of managing strict Agile workflows, learning to prioritize critical components and optimize database structures to guarantee the on-time delivery of robust solutions to clients.

A cornerstone of my technical maturity was architecting a comprehensive Garments & Apparel Management System (Apparel-Flow). Developing this full-scale platform using Angular and Spring Boot provided me with deep insight into structural business logic, supply chain complexities, and resource planning. Combined with modern AI-assisted development environments, I build workflows that are not merely functional, but intelligent, highly scalable, and production-ready from day one.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm drawn to projects that solve complex business problems, particularly full-scale enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems. I enjoy architecting decoupled, multi-tier backends and connecting them to seamless web or mobile interfaces. My background drives me to build solutions that are highly scalable and logically sound.

My primary focus is joining a high-performing engineering team in a full-time capacity, whether remote or onsite. However, I am highly adaptable and always open to discussing freelance or contract projects if they present a strong technical challenge and align with my core engineering stack.

My core backend ecosystem revolves around Java and Spring Boot, utilizing MySQL for database management. On the frontend, I specialize in Angular. For mobile applications, I develop using both the native Android SDK and Flutter for cross-platform solutions.

My optimization strategy adapts to the specific data ecosystem. For relational databases like MySQL and SQLite3, I prioritize strict normalization, strategic indexing, and optimized queries during the architecture phase. For real-time NoSQL solutions like Firebase, I focus on flat data structures and efficient listener management to minimize read/write costs. Regardless of the technology, my goal is to structure the data layer so that applications remain fast and highly scalable.

I follow a disciplined software development lifecycle. It begins with breaking down complex business requirements into actionable logic. I then design the system architecture and database schema. During development, I focus on writing clean, readable code, followed by implementing strict security protocols, thorough testing, and deployment.

I treat security as a foundational architectural layer, not an afterthought. For enterprise applications, I implement Spring Security to lock down the backend, enforce stateless API communication via JWT (JSON Web Tokens) interceptors, and apply strict Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) for safe user-tier management.

The biggest takeaway was the sheer discipline required in an industry-simulated environment. Completing 788 hours of intensive lab modules taught me how to manage strict Agile schedules, handle high-pressure delivery timelines, and consistently produce clean, production-ready code.
